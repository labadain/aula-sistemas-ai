Overview

What students will study

The course moves from symbolic AI and search to supervised and unsupervised learning, then into deep learning, transformers, large language models, and practical deployment workflows.

Prerequisites

Expected preparation

Students should be comfortable with programming, basic algorithms, data structures, and introductory statistics before starting the semester.

Modules

Teaching modules

See full weekly progression
Module 1 AI Foundations

Agents, search, problem-solving, and knowledge representation.

Meetings 1-4 Module 2 Machine Learning

Regression, classification, clustering, and model evaluation with practice.

Meetings 5-10 Module 3 Deep Learning

Neural networks, backpropagation, PyTorch, and computer vision basics.

Meetings 11-18 Module 4 NLP and Generative AI

Embeddings, attention, transformers, and practical LLM-driven applications.

Meetings 19-24 Module 5 AI Systems and Deployment

Data pipelines, app delivery, frameworks, ethics, privacy, and monitoring.

Meetings 25-28 Module 6 Final Project

Proposal, implementation, iteration, and presentation of an end-to-end AI system.
